What to check before cone crusher eccentric bushing burnout

Eccentric bushing failure is often linked to lubrication condition, oil temperature, oil pressure, contamination and operating load. A better inspection record also helps buyers request replacement bronze parts more accurately.

Burnout is rarely just a bushing problem

When a cone crusher eccentric bushing burns out, replacing the bronze bushing alone may not solve the root cause. The maintenance team should also review oil temperature, oil pressure, return screen debris, oil cleanliness, crusher load, CSS setting and visible damage on mating surfaces.

Public maintenance references from Metso and McLanahan both emphasize routine lubrication checks. Metso notes that abnormal lubrication temperature, filter debris and oil condition can reveal internal crusher problems before severe failure.

Warning signs before severe damage

  • Lubrication oil temperature is higher than the normal site baseline.
  • Oil pressure is low, unstable or different from recent operating records.
  • Copper particles appear on the return screen, hydraulic unit filter or tank screen.
  • The crusher shows abnormal noise, vibration, load change or locking.
  • The used bushing has scoring, smearing, local discoloration, adhesion or burned areas.
  • The main shaft, eccentric assembly or mating surface shows scratches or abnormal clearance.
  • The site recently experienced tramp events, chamber packing, overload or long running under poor feed conditions.

Lubrication checks maintenance teams should record

Oil temperature: record supply and return oil temperature during normal operation and before shutdown. A rising trend can point to internal friction, insufficient oil flow or contamination.

Oil pressure: low or fluctuating pressure may relate to pump wear, relief valve issues, oil circuit problems or excessive internal clearance.

Return screen debris: abnormal copper or iron particles should be photographed and kept with the used-part inspection record.

Oil contamination: dust, water, oxidation and fine particles can turn oil into an abrasive medium that accelerates wear on bushings and sleeves.

Breathers and tank condition: blocked breathers and dirty tank screens increase the risk of contamination entering the lubrication circuit.

Operating factors that can shorten bushing life

  • CSS set too tight for the duty
  • Segregated or off-center feed
  • Too many fines in the crushing chamber
  • Incorrect liner configuration or chamber profile
  • Incorrect lubricant grade or insufficient oil flow
  • Continued operation after clearance has become excessive
  • Damaged main shaft, eccentric or related seating surfaces

FAQ

Can a site replace only the eccentric bushing after burnout?

It is risky to inspect only the bushing. Check oil condition, pump performance, oil pressure, oil temperature, filter debris, main shaft surface and chamber operating conditions before restart.

What does copper debris on the filter usually indicate?

Copper debris normally means abnormal wear from a bronze component. The site should stop and inspect the old part surface, lubrication condition and mating clearance.

Is oil temperature required for a bushing quote?

It is not always required for a normal replacement order, but it is very useful when the old bushing failed early, overheated or showed scoring.
